How to use this tool
SaaS Pricing Agent — User Guide
A step-by-step walkthrough for building your complete SaaS pricing model. Work through each tab in order — the outputs from each tab automatically feed into the next.
⏱ ~15 min to complete 💾 Auto-saves as you type 📊 Export to Excel anytime
1
🏗 Deployment Models Start here

Your pricing foundation. Tick the deployment types you offer — Single Tenant (Vendor), Multi-Tenant SaaS, Single Tenant (Client Cloud), or On-Premises. For each active model, enter the Baseline Annual Product License fee ($ per tenant per month). Then fill in Deployment (one-time setup fee), Professional Services, Compliance & Governance, Internal IT / FTE Overhead, and Model Pricing — all in USD. Leave any cost at $0 if it doesn't apply.

💡 Tip: Only tick the models you actually sell — unticked models are excluded from the Pricing Summary and quotes.
💡 Tip: Model Pricing (AI/ML inference costs) only applies to Single Tenant Vendor and Multi-Tenant — it shows N/A for client-managed models.
2
☁️ Azure Pricing Enter infra costs

Enter your Azure infrastructure costs for all 4 environments: Production, Pre-Production, QA, and Dev. Click each environment tab at the top. Inside each one, select Option B (Simple Total) and scroll down to the two coloured input boxes — enter your total monthly Azure bill in the green Single Tenant box and the blue Multi-Tenant box. The tool sums all environments automatically. Use Option A if you have a line-by-line service breakdown.

☁️ Production will be your highest cost environment. Dev and QA should be considerably lower. Single Tenant costs are dedicated per customer; Multi-Tenant costs are shared and divided by your total customer count.
3
👥 Support Staff Team cost model

Select your active support tier using the toggle at the top: 8×5 Standard (Mon–Fri business hours) or 24×7 Enhanced (round-the-clock). The inactive tier grays out but keeps its data. Use Option A to enter individual staff salaries, or Option B to enter a single annual or monthly total. Set your number of customers and target margin % — the tool calculates your cost-per-customer and the support fee you should charge.

👥 These are your COGS (cost to serve), not the price you charge. Your platform license fee is set separately in Step 1. Support cost per customer is automatically included in the Pricing Summary margin calculation.
4
🔒 Security & Compliance Annual costs

Enter your annual security and compliance costs: VAPT (penetration testing), LLM/AI security testing, certification audits (ISO 27001, SOC 2), and SOC monitoring. Toggle any section Not Applicable to zero it out if it doesn't apply yet. Scroll down to also enter Internal Infra & DevOps costs — CI/CD tooling, internal Azure, monitoring platforms, and DevOps staff salaries. All costs are amortised equally across your customer base in the Pricing Summary.

🔒 If you don't have these costs yet, mark everything Not Applicable — the Pricing Summary still works. You can always come back to fill this in later.
5
📊 Pricing Summary ⭐ Main output

This is where everything comes together. Use the Support Tier toggle (8×5 or 24×7) to choose which tier drives the COGS and margin calculation — both rows are visible in every card, but only the active one counts. Each deployment model card shows your full cost stack (Azure infra, support, security, compliance, IT overhead, model pricing) with gross margin calculated automatically. Scroll down to the On-Premises section for hardware and deployment cost modelling.

✅ Margin targets: Aim for 65%+ on hosted models (Single Tenant Vendor, Multi-Tenant), and 55%+ on client-managed deployments.
📊 On-Premises: Scroll down past the model cards to enter hardware, deployment, professional services, and recurring support costs for on-prem deals.
6
📈 Financial Projections

Model MRR/ARR growth over 12, 24, or 36 months. Set starting customers, new customers per month, annual churn rate, and CAC. The tool generates a full month-by-month P&L table and MRR/GP chart automatically based on your live pricing inputs.

7
📦 Pricing Tiers

Build a Good–Better–Best tier structure (e.g. Starter / Professional / Enterprise). Name each tier, set a price, define which features are included, and configure SLA commitments. These tiers feed directly into the Quote Generator when you select a tier for a proposal.

8
💰 Deals, Discounts & ROI

Set discount percentages for annual, 2-year, and 3-year billing commitments. Build a client ROI model by entering what your product replaces (cost savings, staff hours freed, revenue gains) — the tool calculates payback period and 3-year net benefit, which flows into the Quote Generator's ROI section.

9
🔀 Scenario Compare

Compare up to three pricing scenarios side by side — ideal for modelling different markets (UAE vs International), customer sizes, or deployment types. Each scenario has its own independent inputs for customers, support tier, and model, so you can see revenue and margin differences at a glance.

10
📄 Quote Generator Client-ready output

Generate a polished commercial proposal ready to send to a client. Fill in the client company name, contact name and email, your company name, and a short executive summary. Choose which sections to include — pricing breakdown, ROI, SLA commitments, deployment overview, terms. Click Preview Proposal to open a formatted document in a new tab, then use your browser's Print → Save as PDF to download it.

📝 The quote pulls all values from the other tabs automatically — no re-entry needed. Complete Steps 1–5 first for the most accurate proposal. You can also use the AI Advisor tab to ask for pricing strategy recommendations before generating your quote.
🛠 Toolbar Quick Reference
💾 Save All Saves all inputs to your browser and downloads a backup JSON file. Use regularly — especially before closing the browser.
⬇ Export XLS Downloads a full Excel workbook with separate sheets for Deployment Models, Azure, Support, Security, and Pricing Summary.
📂 Import Restores a previously saved JSON backup file — useful for picking up where you left off or sharing a configuration with a colleague.
↺ Reset Resets the current product to factory defaults. This cannot be undone — always export a backup JSON first.
📦 Product Bar — The bar below the toolbar lets you create, clone, and rename multiple product configurations. Use this to keep separate models for different markets or products (e.g. "UAE Enterprise", "International SaaS", "On-Prem Bundle") without overwriting each other.
